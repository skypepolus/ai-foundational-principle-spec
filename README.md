## Purpose

This repository defines the five essential conditions that any foundational principle or protocol must satisfy to serve as a stable base layer for the AI era.
It does not define the principle itself.
Instead, it establishes the criteria — a rigorous, domain‑agnostic test — for evaluating whether a candidate principle is:
• descriptive
• predictive
• generative
• convergent
• recursive
This specification is intended as a reference standard for researchers, engineers, policymakers, and system designers navigating the accelerating complexity of AI‑integrated societies.

---

## Why This Matters

As AI systems become deeply embedded in human decision‑making, infrastructure, governance, and global coordination, the world faces a growing need for:
• a shared conceptual foundation
• a universal reasoning protocol
• a principle that scales across humans and machines
• a framework that avoids ideological fragmentation
• a stable base layer for alignment and cooperation
This repository provides the meta‑framework for identifying such a principle.
It fills a gap that is increasingly visible across AI research, governance, and systems engineering:
the absence of a unifying, structurally sound foundation.

---

## The Five Conditions

A foundational principle must be:

### 1. Descriptive

Accurately reflects how systems — human, machine, and natural — actually behave.

### 2. Predictive

Reliably forecasts stability, collapse, cooperation, conflict, and alignment outcomes.

### 3. Generative

Produces new structures, norms, strategies, and architectures from first principles.

### 4. Convergent

Guides independent agents toward shared structure and stable agreement.

### 5. Recursive

Applies to itself, self‑corrects, and remains coherent under iteration and scaling.
These five conditions form a foundation test for the AI era.
